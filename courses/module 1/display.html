what is a pixel

A pixel is a small square in the display that has three values (red ,green,blue or rgb for short). it can be single transistor

display panel types

CRT (Cathode Ray Tube)

CRTs were commonly used in older computers and TVs. They utilize an electron beam to display pixels. CRTs are known for their fast response time, low cost, and high brightness. However, their main drawback is the risk of burn-in when a static image is displayed for an extended period.

LCD and TN

liquid crystal display are used in modern devices and they are using a white backlight that why colors are very bright and twisted nemantic (tn) are featuring a high refresh rate and low latency but they cannot reproduce accurate colors.

TFT and IPS

LED (light emmiting diod)

LEDs use diodes instead of transistors, allowing them to produce a wider range of colors. However, LEDs can have brightness issues on larger screens, particularly OLEDs, which use organic components. Mini LEDs do not have this problem because they use a large number of LEDs.

frame rate and refresh rate:

Frame rate refers to the number of images displayed on the screen per second. For example, 30 FPS (frames per second) means 30 image frames are shown each second. Refresh rate, on the other hand, is the frequency at which the display updates with new images, measured in Hertz (Hz). For instance, a 120 Hz display refreshes 120 times per second. A low refresh rate can cause stuttering.
