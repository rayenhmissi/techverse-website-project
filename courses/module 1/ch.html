  1. know your budget and your needs

    2. the most important thing to buy anything is knowing what you need and how much money you have or how much you can pay for a computer.don't spend on a high end if you don't need it.

  2. laptop or desktop

    3. desktops are still have more advantages like better hardware , better cooling and they are upgradable but they have cons like they are not portable ,complicated for non tech geeks consumes much power.so laptops are portable and you can use it anywhere all in one package without buying every components and consumes less power but they have limited performance on battery and more heat . so if you prefer the maximum performannce and use computer only at home, choose a desktop .if you prefer portability and mobility and don't need the full power of the machine,you should go for a laptop.

  3. other considerations

    4. you should look at online reviews and don't rely on specs on paper. you should also don't buy older high end hardware rather than modern one if it is unsupported for softwares that you need because they are not secure and have more heat and dead battery if it is a laptop.they are also expensive if they are not available.so you need to be careful .old high end hardware is not always powerful.it is only powerful for its generation and mid range modern hardware have competitive results and supported by OEMs. for example,in 2024 ,a midrange cpu can be better than high end cpu from 2021.

  4. choice is based on usage
    1. school

      2. for schools you don't need the highest end hardware at all . if you want desktop mid range components like intel core i5 is sufficient but if you study computer science ,you need at least 16gb ram and 512gb and if you don't play demanding games in break time integrated gpu or low end dedicated are enough.if you study anything related to 3d you need dedicated gpu like nvidia rtx gpus and more ram and storage.in laptop side,you can afford a laptop with same requirements as a desktop but you should consider the weight,size,fan noise,heat and battery life.because you don't want to disturb your classmate with fan noise and high tempetures and don''t want your laptop to turn off before completing the the course ,specs on paper don't tell you these aspects.

    2. business

      3. for a business office like working in a bank or anything related you can consider a mide range desktop or thin and light laptop with 16gb ram recommanded because web browwsers need a decent amount of ram these days.you need an ssd drive for fast boot time and quick app launch.you need a laptop with decent battery life and minimal fan noise and heat with modern design.you don't need a dedicated gpu.

    3. software developers

      4. choosing a laptop for software developement depends on what you develop .if you are a web developer you need to focus on ram because web browwsers needs a lot of ram at least 16gb.mid range cpu is enough and you don't a powerful gpu
      if you are apple developer who develops apps for apple products like iphone you need a mac especially macs with apple silicon but you should also begin from 16gb with 512gb ssd and macbook air is not the best for heavy workloads.
      if you are windows app developer you can go with 16gb and i7 or ryzen 7 cpus.you don't need a high end gpu.
      if you are an android developer you need 16gb ram and a decent mid range cpu like i5 or i7 or ryzen 5 or ryzen 7.
      if you are a game developer you need a powerful gpu like nvidia rtx or amd alternative like rx gpu,32gb ram ,ssd drive and a powerful cpu.
      if you are ai developer you need a powerful gpu or an npu and a powerful cpu and a lot of ram at least 32gbb ram.

    4. if you are a designer you need a powerful cpu like i7 or ryzen 7 and gpu like rtx 40 series or rx 70series with a decent amount of ram at least 32gb and you need a color accurate display
